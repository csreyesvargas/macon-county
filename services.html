Permits & Licenses

The Macon County Department of Planning, Permitting & Development is comprised of several divisions including building inspections, environmental services and the county planner. The Building Inspections office is authorized by the North Carolina General Statutes to enforce the North Carolina State Building Codes.

Types of Permits

  • Building Permits
  • Electrical Permits
  • Plumbing Permits
  • Mechanical Permits (Heating & AC)
  • Sign Permits

How to Apply

Visit the Department of Planning, Permitting & Development at the Human Services Building, 1834 Lakeside Dr., Franklin, NC 28734 or call (828) 349-2073.

Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Director: Jack Morgan - (828) 349-2170

Tax Information

The Macon County Tax Office is responsible for listing, appraising, and assessing all real and personal property within the county.

Property Taxes

  • Current tax rate: $0.3475 per $100 value
  • Bills mailed in July each year
  • Due date: January 5
  • Interest begins January 6

Payment Options

  • In person at the Tax Office
  • By mail
  • Online payment available
  • Drop box available 24/7

Contact: (828) 349-2100

Health Services

The Macon County Public Health Center serves many facets of our community. From the well being of children and adults to the environment, our many services are broad-based.

Vision: To be the model for best practices in Public Health.

Mission: To promote, protect, and enhance the health of all people and the environment.

Clinical Services

  • Immunizations
  • Family Planning
  • Maternal Health / Prenatal Care
  • STD Testing & Treatment
  • TB Screening
  • Child Health Clinics
  • WIC Program
  • Dental Health (Molar Roller)
  • Integrated Primary Care
  • International Travel Services

Environmental Health

  • Well Permits & Water Testing
  • Septic System Permits
  • Food & Lodging Inspections
  • Swimming Pool Inspections
  • Animal Bite Investigations
  • Daycare & School Inspections

Other Services

  • Care Coordination for Children (CC4C)
  • Pregnancy Care Management
  • Community Preparedness
  • Health Education
  • Nutrition & Diabetes Education
